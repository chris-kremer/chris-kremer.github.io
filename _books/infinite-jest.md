A novel about entertainment, addiction, and the strange loops of desire. Wallace constructs a near-future North America where a film is so pleasurable it is lethal, and where the structures meant to help us — therapy, AA, family — are revealed as both inadequate and essential.

What makes it worth the weight is the prose itself: dense, funny, tender, and merciless. There's a section on a halfway house in Boston that I think about more than almost anything else I've read. The footnotes aren't a gimmick — they're a formal argument about the relationship between text and attention.

Read with a wiki open for the chronology, and don't be discouraged by the first hundred pages. The book earns everything it asks of you.
